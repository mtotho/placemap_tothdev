The BAAT wants to improve the health of both its downtown and its residences, mainly by improving the walkability, bikeability and livability of downtown.

To create a more vibrant and inviting downtown, BAAT has identified a need to:

  • Slow down traffic on East Park Avenue and East Commercial Avenue
  • Give motorists something to catch their attention as they pass through
  • Reduce the barrier effect that downtown streets pose to walking and biking and vibrancy
  • Create a strategy for filling in vacant or demolished lots
  • Provide more destinations downtown via Placemaking
    • Activate existing places such as Kennedy Commons and Durst Park
    • Create new places and plazas

As part of its initiative to create a more vibrant downtown, BAAT successfully applied to the New Mobility West program for technical assistance. From October 8th through the 11th, a team led by the Sonoran Institute and including the Project for Public conducted a series of interviews, walking and intersection audits and a placemapping exercise. All were done with the intent of engaging Anacondans in identifying the assets, detriments and opportunities in adding walking, biking and placemaking downtown.

This online placemapping exercise is intended to provide an opportunity for Anacondans to continue the conversation about our future.
